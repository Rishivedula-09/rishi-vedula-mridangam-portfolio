# Rhythm, Mathematics, and Structured Thinking

## A Long-Term Exploration Through Mridangam

### Overview
This repository documents my long-term learning journey in Carnatic percussion (mridangam) and my growing interest in exploring rhythm through structured thinking, mathematics, and computer science.
I have been learning mridangam for more than eight years.
In my early years, I focused mainly on technique, timing, and performance. Over time, I became increasingly curious about how rhythmic patterns are structured, how they align within tala cycles, and how complex rhythmic sequences are built from smaller repeating units.
This project serves as a structured record of that exploration â€” combining music, analytical thinking, and long-term learning.

---

## Project Purpose
The goal of this project is to explore rhythm not only as a performance art but also as a structured system connected to mathematics, logic, and computational thinking.

Through this portfolio, I am studying:
      -  Construction of rhythmic patterns
      -  Tala structure and alignment
      -  Korvai design and resolution
      -  Pattern symmetry and grouping
      -  Mathematical representation of rhythm
      -  Algorithmic thinking through musical structure

Rather than viewing music and academic subjects separately, this project reflects how musical training can strengthen structured and logical thinking.

----
## Why I Started This Project
After many years of learning rhythm through practice and performance, I began to notice that rhythmic structures often resemble mathematical and logical systems.

For example:
      -  Patterns follow fixed counts and repeat in cycles
      -  Groupings create symmetry and variation
      -  Korvais require precise alignment and resolution
      -  Different pattern lengths interact in predictable ways

This led me to start documenting my learning in a more structured way â€” not only to improve as a percussionist, but also to better understand rhythm as a system involving structure, counting, and reasoning.
